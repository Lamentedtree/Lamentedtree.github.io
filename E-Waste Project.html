What is E-waste?

E-waste refers to electronic waste, which includes discarded electronic devices such as computers, mobile phones, televisions, and more. These devices contain hazardous materials that can harm the environment and human health if not disposed of properly.

Impact of E-waste

Improper disposal of e-waste leads to pollution of air, water, and soil. The toxic chemicals present in electronic devices can contaminate natural resources and pose a threat to ecosystems. Recycling and responsible e-waste management are essential to reduce environmental damage and recover valuable resources.

E-waste statistics

Here are some E-waste statistics according to the Global E-waste Monitor 2020 report

  • Only 17.4% of the 2019 e-waste generated was recycled.
  • The world generated a record 53.6 million metric tons of e-waste in 2019.
  • The value of raw materials present in e-waste is around $57 billion.
  • The top three countries with the highest e-waste generation in 2019 were China (10.1 million), the United States (6.9 million), and India (3.2 million).

How to Recycle E-waste

Recycling e-waste involves the proper collection, sorting, and processing of electronic devices to extract valuable materials and dispose of hazardous substances safely. Many countries have established specialized recycling centers or programs for e-waste disposal. It's important to find certified e-waste recyclers to ensure environmentally friendly practices.
